Sea Level Rise Assessment

Sea-level rise (SLR) is widely discussed as a major consequence of global warming. This positive correlation is a major concern for communities close to the shoreline. GIS models allow local governments to predict and prepare for marine innundation. The Sea Level Rise Viewer, developed by the NOAA Office for Coastal Management, is the model which we scrutinized to find the variables left unaccounted.

Geospatial data was collected from various sources: archaeological sites were used as landmarks for determining stable land surfaces of the Holocene period; old maps collected from the USGS and the Library of Virginia allows us to visualize coastal geography over the last few hundred years; and field work was conducted to ground-truth the model.
