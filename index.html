Sea Level Rise Assessment

Sea-level rise (SLR) is widely discussed as a major consequence of global warming. This positive correlation is a major concern for communities close to the shoreline. GIS models allow local governments to predict and prepare for marine innundation. The Sea Level Rise Viewer, developed by the NOAA Office for Coastal Management, is the model which we scrutinized to find the variables left unaccounted.

Geospatial data was collected from various sources: archaeological sites were used as landmarks for determining stable land surfaces of the Holocene period; old maps collected from the USGS and the Library of Virginia allows us to visualize coastal geography over the last few hundred years; and field work was conducted to ground-truth the model.

GIS Specialist

Virginia Dept. of Historic Resources

January 2014 (Contract Employment)

Summary: Dr. Michael Barber, the State Archaeologist for the Virginia Department of Historic Resources, was applying for a state grant to preserve Werowocomoco. This archaeological site is located along the York River alongside Purtan Bay. This location was the primary village of Paramount Chief Powhatan. Excavations have demonstrated that the site was occupied from AD 1400 and that the village was divided by landscape manipulation into secular and sacred areas.

Responsibility: Publicly available satellite imagery, historic topographic maps, and geospatial data provided essential information to assess the amount of landform change that occured. Geospatial techniques (georeferencing and GPS field assessment) were performed to illustrate and map the coastal morpholgy change. A geomorpholgy report on these measurements helped outline a case for funding the preservation of this histroical site.
